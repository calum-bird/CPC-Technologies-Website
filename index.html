A paradigm shift in personal computers

CloudPC redefines what a personal computer can be.








Upgrade in seconds.


Your CloudPC can be upgraded at the press of a button to unleash up to 8x the compute power. Gone are the days of day-long upgrades. Stop paying for the max workload requirement - pay for the minimum. Downgrade at any time. You just need to restart.









Only pay for what you use.


You only pay for your CloudPC while it is running. Pay a small fee for upgrades, but never pay for downgrades. Storage is billed monthly, it's usage doesn't matter. Don't need your computer for a couple hours? Don't worry. You just need to shutdown.









Connect from any device


Your CloudPC can be access through any device with a web browser. It behaves exactly like the Windows PC you're used to. Purchase and install programs the same way - there is no learning curve. Connect instantly from any online device. You just need to login.

If you would like to just get an understanding on how much CloudPC will cost for you, please feel free to use the calculator linked below!


Estimate your costs



Setup fee - $25

We charge a small $25 setup fee to ensure that we can cover internal costs when we first launch your new CloudPC. Of this fee, $10 goes towards credits immediately available for using your CloudPC upon setup.

Storage - 10¢/GB/mo

Like a traditional computer's hard drive (or SSD), CloudPC requires it's own internal storage. We charge 10¢ per GB each month. For example, if you would like 128GB of internal storage, it would cost $12.80/month.


Upgrades - $2.50

We charge a $2.50 fee each time you upgrade your CloudPC. For example, if you change your plan from 8GB RAM and 2 vCPU's to 16GB RAM and 4 vCPU's, you will be charged $2.50 from your credits, and usage will be billed at the new rate.

Usage - starts at 25¢/hr

With CloudPC, how much you pay is dictated by how much you use. There are four "plans" each with different resources for different use-cases. Each subsequent plan has double the resources as the one before it, and costs twice as much.
